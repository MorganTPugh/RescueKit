# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Two equally primary audiences:

**Foster volunteers** — individuals at home creating materials for one specific pet they're currently fostering. They open RescueKit with a phone full of photos and a story to tell; they need to go from "blank" to "shareable flyer" in minutes, without a learning curve.

**Rescue org coordinators / staff** — admins at small rescue organizations managing multiple pets and building materials semi-regularly. They care about looking professional, staying on-brand for their org, and getting through the workflow efficiently.

Both groups share a core context: they're doing this on behalf of an animal they care about, often in their spare time. The tool should feel like it's helping them — not demanding their attention.

## Product Purpose

RescueKit is a free toolkit for animal rescue volunteers, fosters, and small rescue organizations to:
- Create print/share-ready adoption flyers and posters (with AI-generated bios via Gemini)
- Access a structured foster care guide
- Build rescue needs flyers to request supplies and support
- Discover and track rescue grants

Success looks like: a foster parent creates a beautiful, professional-quality flyer in minutes — and a pet gets adopted because of it.

## Brand Personality

Capable, efficient, trustworthy. The feel of a Canva or Adobe Express — approachable enough that anyone can pick it up, capable enough that the output looks polished and professional. The warmth in RescueKit comes from the animal's story and the poster output, not from the UI performing emotion at the user.

Three words: **Grounded. Capable. Caring.**

## Anti-references

- **Overly kiddie / crayon-cute** — cartoon fonts, bubble shapes, primary-color overload. The tool must feel credible to a rescue org coordinator, not like a children's activity app.
- **Sad-charity guilt-trip aesthetic** — heavy, dark, ASPCA-commercial energy. RescueKit should feel hopeful and energizing, not solemn.
- **Generic SaaS chrome** — cold, startup-dashboard-y, metric cards everywhere, feature-announcement banners. This is a craft tool for people who care about animals.

## Design Principles

1. **Tools that trust their users** — don't over-explain or over-decorate. Get out of the way so the foster can focus on the animal's story, not the interface. One task, clear path.
2. **Animals are the star** — the tool is a stage, not a performer. Reduce UI noise; let the content (photos, bios, flyers) breathe and take center stage.
3. **Warmth through craft, not decoration** — emotional resonance comes from the quality of the output and the care behind it, not from UI ornamentation or emoji-heavy copy.
4. **Approachable expertise** — feel like Canva: anyone can use it, but the result looks polished enough to share with confidence. Never dumbed down, never intimidating.
5. **Efficient compassion** — every interaction should be fast and clear. These users are volunteers; their time is precious and already given.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

No formal WCAG target, but the baseline expectation is: solid contrast, clear labels, keyboard-navigable forms, readable type at all sizes. The tool should be usable without strain — rescue volunteers span a wide age range and device quality.
